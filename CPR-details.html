CPR

When a person's heart stops beating, immediate action is requiredâ€”this is where CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation) becomes crucial.


CPR is a life-saving technique that combines chest compressions and rescue breaths to restore circulation and breathing in a person who has gone into cardiac arrest.


Performing CPR quickly and effectively can significantly increase the chances of survival. Proper chest compressions ensure that blood continues to flow to the brain and other vital organs.


Chest compressions should be performed at a steady rhythm, with sufficient depth to effectively pump blood. Rescue breaths provide oxygen to the lungs, which is critical for maintaining life until professional medical assistance arrives.


Knowing how to perform CPR is an essential skill that can save lives during emergencies. It is important to undergo training and keep your skills updated to respond effectively when the need arises.


Organizations like the American Heart Association provide CPR training to ensure that more people are equipped to help in critical situations.


When performed correctly, CPR can sustain life until advanced medical care is available, making it a vital first-aid procedure for cardiac emergencies.
